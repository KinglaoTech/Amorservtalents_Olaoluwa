Our Focus: Africa

Between 2020 and 2021, the pool of African professional developers increased by 3.8%, from 690,000 to 716,000.

Despite these changes, multinational corporations have yet to fully capitalize on the availability of African digital talent With Africa's working-age population expected to reach 1.3 billion by 2050, it's tough to justify ignoring Africa as a source of tech talent for the rest of the globe

Recruiting African talent to work remotely has the potential to generate billions of dollars in revenue for global businesses while also unlocking the potential and increasing awareness of millions of young Africans.

Why Africa?

The world is facing a tech talent shortage. There were 920,000 vacant information technology (IT) positions in the United States alone in 2020, with fewer than 50,000 computer science graduates to fill over 500,000 roles. In the same year, 79 percent of global CEOs expressed concern about digital talent shortages, while 61 percent of global HR experts predicted that tech talent shortages will be their largest difficulty in 2022. By 2030, the situation is expected to worsen.
